What is Valorant?

Game Rules

It’s a 5v5 multiplayer first-person shooter (FPS) where one team attacks and the other defends. The main game mode, Search and Destroy, is very similar to CS:GO. The attacking team’s goal is to plant a bomb (called a spike) and have it detonate, while the defending team tries to avoid that. Regardless of whether the spike is planted or not, if a squad is wiped out before any other victory condition is met, the opposing squad will win.

  • Two teams of five players compete to complete objectives across various maps.
  • Each player selects a unique agent with special abilities to contribute to their team's strategy.
  • Rounds consist of attacking and defending teams, with attackers aiming to plant a spike and defenders aiming to prevent it.
  • Teams earn currency by completing objectives and eliminating opponents, which can be used to purchase weapons and abilities.
  • The first team to win a set number of rounds, or eliminate the opposing team, wins the match.

    • What platforms is Valorant on?

    NValorant is a PC-exclusive game right now. Valorant lead game designer Trevor Romleski confirmed on a Twitch stream that the “focus right now is on PC.” Romleski also said, however, that the team is “open to exploring new opportunities for other platforms.”

    For the time being, the only way to play Valorant is on PC. We’re assuming that if the game is successful, though, it’ll likely be ported to PS4 and Xbox One in the future. Maybe we’ll even see a PS5 and Xbox Series X release..
